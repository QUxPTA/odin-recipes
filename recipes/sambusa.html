Sambusa ya Nyama (Meat Samosa) Recipe

Description

Nothing more delicious like the Kenyan meaty samosa! The meaty samosa serves as a breakfast item and a whole day snack. It can be enjoyed by the entire family.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg minced beef
  • 30 g coriander, fresh
  • 1 stem (356 g) leek, unpeeled, raw
  • 1 clove (5 g) garlic, unpeeled, raw
  • ¼ tsp. (1 g) chilli pepper, fresh, raw
  • 3 cups (475 g) wheat flour, refined
  • ½ tsp. (2 g) salt, iodized
  • 1¼ cups (264 g) water
  • 3 cups (572 g) cooking oil

Preparation 1 hour | Cooking Time 1 hour | Makes 28

Meat Filling:

  1. Put the meat in a pan over a fire. Stir continuously.
  2. When the juices start oozing out from the meat, cover to cook.
  3. Finely chop the leek, coriander and crash the garlic. Mix them together in a bowl.
  4. Add salt and chilli to the meat after about 10 minutes.
  5. Continue stirring and crushing the meat to prevent it from forming balls.
  6. Cook the meat until it dries.
  7. Add the leek, garlic and coriander after 4 minutes and stir.
  8. Remove from the fire after 1 minute.
  9. Let the meat cool before using it to fill the casings.

Breading (Samosa Pocket Preparation):

  1. Put 2 ½ cups flour in a bowl and add 1 cup cold water. Mix into a medium soft dough.
  2. Turn dough onto a flat surface and knead thor- oughly until smooth.
  3. Make a thick long roll, cut into 9 pieces and make the pieces into small balls.
  4. Roll out the balls, one at a time into desired thickness.
  5. Flour the surface, slightly oil the top of each rolled-out ball and stack them on top of each other. Continue until all the pieces are placed.
  6. Stitch the edges by poking light holes.
  7. Roll them out together to one large round piece.
  8. Heat a shallow pan and toast the rolled dough for 2 minutes per side. If the shallow pan is too small, cut the rolled-out dough to fit.
  9. Toast each side lightly, peeling off each layer after toasting until all the 9 pieces are separated.
  10. Cut triangular shapes on each separated piece; fold each of the triangular pieces into an enve- lope and seal the edges with a preparation of wheat flour paste.

Assembly (Making paste):

  • The paste is used to seal the envelopes.
  • Mix ¼ cup of water and ½ cup of flour to make a thick paste.
  • Make the casings using the paste to bond and seal the edges.

Filling the Samosa:

  1. Spoon the cooled meat filling into the casings and seal off the edges using the paste.
  2. Do not overfill the casings to prevent it any tears or rips on them.

Cooking:

  1. Heat the remaining oil in a deep pan up to 170°C or about 6 minutes or until the oil bubbles when a pinch of dough is dropped in the oil.
  2. Carefully, deep each samosa into the hot oil and cook while turning until golden brown on both sides.
  3. Remove from the hot oil making sure to let any excess oil drain back into the pan.
  4. Place onto paper towels to drain any excess oil.
  5. Repeat the process until all the samosas are cooked.
  6. Serve.
